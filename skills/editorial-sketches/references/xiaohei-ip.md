# Xiaohei Character

## Character Definition

Xiaohei is Ian's recurring visual character for editorial article illustrations.

By default, every image should include Xiaohei. Xiaohei is not a mascot, sticker, or cute decoration. Xiaohei is an absurd worker seriously participating in the system's operation.

## Appearance

- Small solid-black creature.
- White dot eyes.
- Thin legs, sometimes thin arms.
- The body can be a cylinder, black bean, black box, funnel, shadow, hole, or black mass inside a machine.
- The outline is slightly irregular and hand-drawn.
- The expression is blank, dazed, calm, and serious.

## Personality

- Very serious, while doing something slightly absurd.
- Feels like a low-key system operator.
- Dry humor, no cuteness.
- A little awkward, but not stupid.
- Feels like it is genuinely responsible for a job inside a whiteboard sketch.

## Common Responsibilities

Make Xiaohei perform the core action:

- Carrying material.
- Pulling wires to gather information sources.
- Getting stuck in a breakpoint.
- Operating a "judgment" lever inside a machine.
- Becoming a filtering funnel.
- Cutting open a "material fish".
- Stamping handoff language.
- Leading a handoff path.
- Holding a warning sign while looking at pitfalls.
- Reaching out from a hole but failing to catch content.
- Building, bridging, opening doors, sorting, or recording as part of the system.

## Prohibited

- Do not make Xiaohei an overly cute mascot.
- Do not make Xiaohei a children's cartoon character.
- Do not give Xiaohei complex clothing, meme expressions, or shiny eyes.
- Do not let Xiaohei simply stand in a corner and watch.
- Do not let Xiaohei overwhelm the structural explanation.
- Do not make Xiaohei too commercial, too round, or too polished.

## Quality Standard

If the image's core metaphor still works completely after removing Xiaohei, Xiaohei is too decorative. Rewrite the prompt so Xiaohei becomes the action subject.
