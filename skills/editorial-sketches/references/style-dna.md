# Style DNA

## One Sentence

Pure white, minimal, hand-drawn, spacious, restrained, strange, product-sketch-like, with handwritten annotations in the source language and clear structure without feeling like a manual.

It should feel like an explanatory sketch casually drawn on a blank sheet of paper by someone who has spent a long time working with AI, product design, design systems, and developer tools.

## Required

- 16:9 horizontal inline article illustration.
- Pure white background: no beige, warm gray, paper texture, gradients, shadows, noise, or vintage paper effect.
- Mainly black hand-drawn line art: thin lines, slightly wobbly, not mechanical, not vector-clean, not heavy outlines.
- Lots of empty space: the main subject should occupy about 40%-60% of the canvas, with at least 35% blank white space. Ideally leave one quiet open area.
- Sparse handwritten annotations in the source language: at most 5-8 labels. Keep each label extremely short, ideally 1-4 words or the equivalent in the source language.
- One image should explain only one core action, structure, state, or metaphor.
- Express the structure naturally. Do not write the structure type on the image.

## Colors

- Black: main line art, character, frames, structure, main text, and main objects.
- Red: emphasis, problems, emotional points, key reminders, and results.
- Orange: primary flow, path, arrows, automation movement, or A-to-B movement.
- Blue: secondary notes, mental state, system state, second-layer explanation, AI/assistant/automation hints.

Blue is not required in every image. Keep color restrained; less is better than more.

## Absolutely Avoid

- No commercial illustration.
- No PPT infographic.
- No formal flowchart.
- No course slide.
- No cute cartoon poster.
- No children's illustration.
- No complex architecture diagram.
- No polished flat illustration.
- No techy UI aesthetic.
- No real app screenshots.
- No complex background, gradients, shadows, or textures.
- Do not explain every node.
- Do not put type titles such as "Workflow", "System Architecture", "Pitfalls", or "Roadmap" in the top-left corner.

## Aesthetic Direction

Aim for strange, creative, interesting, clean, fresh, and imaginative.

Avoid cute, childish, overly complex, or stiff.
